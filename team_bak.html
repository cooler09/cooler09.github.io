Team

While our offices are spread throughout Pennsylvania, our small, family owned roots remain as we support our clients throughout the industries. Read more about what makes each office unique below.

Headquarters

TCG’s Hamburg office is the main headquarters for The Crossroads Group. As the largest of the three offices, they offer a full array of all our services. What started in Quakertown, PA, has strategically relocated for easy accessibility to land development and telecom sites throughout the Pennsylvania.
The culture of this office, and TCG as a whole, is that of many family owned businesses. We work diligently with our clients to achieve their goals in a timely and cost-effective manner. We maintain this ethic for our teams as to encourage and improve upon individual skills and improve quality of life. The Hamburg office enjoys friendly competitions and social events. They also support the local community through sponsorships and volunteer time for events such as the Hamburg-er Festival, Art of the Brew Festival, The King Frost Parade, and collaborating with Hamburg Area High School’s mentorship program.

Paxinos Branch

TCG’s Paxinos office was first established in Elysburg, PA, after the acquisition of Richard C. Parsons Land Surveying in November 2011. This team has since grown and moved just down the road to the Bebenek ‘Crossroads’ Plaza in Paxinos, a truly fitting location! The Paxinos team offers full engineering services in addition to the mainstay surveying that got this office started. On the occasional Friday, you can find this group taking a break from the typical day to day tasks by enjoying egg sandwiches for breakfast and watching movie trailers. As can be said for all TCG offices, we have an open-door policy. However, in the Paxinos community, more so than others, friends, neighbors, and clients actively use it to stop by and have a good, old-fashioned discussion and we love it!

Pittsburgh Branch

TCG’s Pittsburgh office was established in November 2018. Although this office started out as a small remote group of 3, after adding a few new team members, they are now officially established in Bridgeville, PA. While the Pittsburgh office specializes in wireless telecommunications, their abilities extend to fiber engineering and data collection/field audits in the tri-state area of Western PA, Central/East Ohio, and West Virginia. Additionally, the team works to support both the survey and land development services in Hamburg and Paxinos. TCG Pittsburgh believes that the best way to break up work tension is a visit from our furry friends or a potluck style lunch. We whole heartedly believe and support the notion that happy employees willingly go the extra mile to make happy clients.

Joshua D. Hoagland

Vice President

Brother of one, second to none, our Vice President, Joshua Hoagland excels at fighting black bears (albeit under 300 lbs). Josh joined the company in 2008 after graduating Johns Hopkins University with a degree in Civil Engineering and worked his way up to obtaining his Professional Engineer license, managing projects, and eventually becoming VP. When he is not ridding the wilderness of dangerous bears, he likes to design subdivisions and commercial land developments. He has worked all throughout bear territory including Ohio, West Virginia, Maryland, and New York to name a few of our service areas outside of Pennsylvania. (He has yet to win a fight with a Grizzly Bear. It’s a work in progress.)

Nicholas Duchan

P.L.S., Director of Land Surveying

Nick has been with The Crossroads Group since October 2014 and has more than 24 years of experience in all phases of surveying required to support engineers designing local roads, residential subdivision, commercial, & land development projects, as well as boundary resolutions & construction stakeout. His experience also includes horizontal & vertical control for aerial mapping, boundary retracement & topography surveys, construction stakeout for commercial, residential, & industrial project. His office experience includes project management, boundary calculations, final plan preparation, & calculations for field crews’ construction stakeout. As Director of Land Surveying, Nick is responsible for the growth & maintenance of the survey department, project management, client expansion, & contract negotiations. He also enjoys hiking, kayaking, & mountaineering and has witnessed Josh’s bear fighting skills.
